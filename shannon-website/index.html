About Shannon

Greetings from Portland. My name is Shannon Nutt and I completed my coursework for a PhD in History at the University of Nevada Las Vegas under the direction of Dr. Deirdre Clemente. My research spans the 20th century with a focus on material culture and public history. I am passionate about all aspects of public history including exhibitions, preservation, memory, research, and public outreach. My public history training includes curation, exhibition design, collections management, preserving local history, collecting oral histories, and political consulting.

I have advanced research skills honed in two Master’s degrees programs. As a PhD student, I am well versed in archival research and skilled at condensing a large amount of information into a palatable amount. I also spent several years as a teacher and a graduate teaching assistant, and have done numerous professional presentations, demonstrating that I can share research and leadership skills in a way that is accessible and useful to varied audiences. I am a confident writer, able to present something in a succinct and structured fashion. My educational background has trained me to be confident working with different groups of people in a variety of situations, a skill that is extremely useful in a variety of situations.
